FAQ

  • • Where is the venue?

    - Gowanus!

  • • Eww, isn't Gowanus gross?

    - Once upon a time, yes! Gowanus is actually a superfund site. And while it can still be a little gross at times, it's now filled with lots of fun restaurants, bars, and other random activities. Gowanus even got its own Whole Foods a couple years ago--so you know it's made it.

  • • Where should I stay?

    - We encourage you to stay in Brooklyn (see fun things to do below), but we realize this might not be for everyone. Not to worry! There are plenty of great hotels in Manhattan. It's very easy to get to Gowanus from Manhattan (20 minute cab from Lower Manhattan, 30 minute cab from Midtown). Greg strongly encourages you not stay in Times Square. It is touristy, overpriced, crowded, and dirty.

  • • We're from out of town. Where should we fly into?

    - LGA and JFK are your best bets. You can also fly into EWR (Newark). If you go the EWR route, I recommend taking an Uber to your hotel (NJ cab drivers aren't very good).

  • • What should I wear?

    - This isn't for Greg to decide.

  • • Where is the Brooklyn Bridge?

    - The Brooklyn Bridge spans DUMBO to Chinatown. From the Brooklyn Brige Marriott or Hilton it's about a 15 minute walk. You can get on the bridge here. It is a little tricky to find--it's actually underneath the bridge itself. Look for people selling worthless trinkets or hot dogs.

  • • Where is Williamsburg?

    - Williamsburg is the part of Brooklyn featured on TV shows such as Girls. It's a part of North Brooklyn. We don't really go there, so unfortunately we can't tell you much about it.

  • • Any suggestions on fun things to do while we're in Brooklyn?

    - Yeah!

    • Brooklyn Heights Promenade - Looking for a nice stroll? Head to the Promenade. Walk to the end of Remsen Street and then walk along as you enjoy beautiful views of Lower Manhattan.
    • NY Transit Museum - Fun museum teaching you the history of the New York City subway system.
    • Brooklyn Museum - Art museum located on the border of Prospect Park.
    • Brooklyn Bridge Park - Check out the bridge and take some pictures of lower Manhattan.
    • Prospect Park - Run or bike the loop, visit the farmer's market on Saturdays, or just take a stroll. Prospect Park is always good for some people watching!
    • Black Mountain Wine House - Looking for a glass of wine or cocktail before the wedding? Head to Black Mountain where you can cozy up next to New York's #1 rated fireplace (quite the distinction) with a drink. Bonus: It's 2 blocks from the venue.
    • Other Half Brewing - Greg's favorite brewery! Other Half is a bit more bare-bones but serves up some great brews! 5 minute cab ride from the venue.
    • Three's Brewing - Great place to have a lunch and dinner and enjoy a wide variety of craft adult beverages. 5 blocks from the venue.
    • Royal Palms Suffleboard Club - A fan favorite. Royal Palms is a lot of fun! Good for friends and family alike. It's also on the same block as the venue. Plan ahead, the wait for a lane can be pretty long!
